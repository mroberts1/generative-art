# Philip Galanter

Philip Galanter is a theorist, artist, and programmer specializing in generative art and complexity science. He is best known for formulating the most widely cited definition of generative art (2003), which emphasizes systems-based creative practices across digital and non-digital domains.

## Key Contributions

### Definition of Generative Art (2003)

Galanter's seminal definition — that generative art refers to any art practice using a system set into motion with some degree of autonomy — has become foundational to the field. His framework explicitly rejects the conflation of "generative art" and "computer art," arguing instead for a unified theory of systems-based art that transcends technological boundaries.

### Complexity Science Framework

Galanter advocates for [[complexity science]] as the theoretical lens through which to understand and compare all manner of generative art systems. This approach ties together historical and contemporary practices, from the [[Jacquard loom]] to [[machine learning]] based art.

### Clarifications and Distinctions

Galanter has published multiple works clarifying common misconceptions about generative art:
- Distinguishing weak autonomy from philosophical autonomy
- Separating rule-based art from generative art
- Establishing that generative systems predate computers
