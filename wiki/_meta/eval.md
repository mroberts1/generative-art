# Foundry Evaluation

Stable questions that should be answerable from this wiki. Re-run on every `/foundry-eval`. Thin/missing findings feed back into `wiki/_meta/index.md` as Open Questions and Candidates.

**Ratings:**
- `strong` — wiki has the concepts and they cite ≥2 tiered sources
- `thin` — wiki has the concepts but sourcing is weak or single-tier
- `missing` — wiki can't answer

Questions are grouped by what they probe. Don't reorder or renumber — that breaks comparability across runs. Add new questions at the bottom.

---

## Definitional / scope (does the wiki know what it's about?)

**Q1. What is generative art, and what distinguishes it from adjacent practices like algorithmic design, procedural generation, or computer art?**
Probes: core definition, boundary-drawing. Expected early rating: thin (Galanter covers it but no second source yet).

**Q2. Why does the field insist generative art predates computers, and what non-digital examples demonstrate this?**
Probes: historical scope, Jacquard/LeWitt/Cage-type precursors. Expected early rating: thin.

**Q3. What role does complexity science play in generative-art theory, and which practitioners or theorists argue for it most strongly?**
Probes: theoretical framework, attribution. Expected early rating: thin.

## Historical / lineage (does the wiki connect the dots across time?)

**Q4. Who are the key figures of first-generation computer art (roughly 1960s–early 1970s), and what distinguishes their approaches?**
Probes: Nake, Nees, Noll, Molnar, Mohr, Csuri, Harmon, etc. Expected early rating: missing.

**Q5. How did plotter-based practice differ between Europe (Stuttgart School, Paris) and the US in the 1960s–1970s?**
Probes: regional lineages, institutional context. Expected early rating: missing.

**Q6. What precedents from conceptual art, Fluxus, or systems art inform contemporary generative practice?**
Probes: pre-1960s and non-digital precursors, cross-movement influence. Expected early rating: missing.

## Technical / craft (does the wiki go deeper than surface?)

**Q7. What are the major algorithmic families used in generative art (e.g. L-systems, cellular automata, flow fields, reaction-diffusion, genetic algorithms), and what aesthetic tendencies does each produce?**
Probes: technique taxonomy with aesthetic consequences, not just a list. Expected early rating: missing.

**Q8. How has the move from plotter to screen to blockchain-native (long-form generative, fx(params)) changed what artists can make and how the work circulates?**
Probes: medium history, market/circulation, recent platforms. Expected early rating: missing.

## Contested / critical (does the wiki hold open the disputes?)

**Q9. Where do practitioners and theorists genuinely disagree about what counts as generative art — and what's the strongest argument on each side?**
Probes: conflict handling, `Contested:` concepts. Expected early rating: missing (and the wiki should probably have at least one Contested concept within the first year).

**Q10. What has generative AI (diffusion models, LLMs) changed, broken, or exposed in the generative-art tradition — and which voices in the community argue it belongs vs. doesn't?**
Probes: present-tense debate, the wiki's ability to track a live controversy without collapsing it. Expected early rating: missing.
