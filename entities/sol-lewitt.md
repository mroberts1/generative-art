# Sol LeWitt

Sol LeWitt was a conceptual artist who used combinatorial systems to generate complex artworks from simple components. His work represents an early example of generative art practice in the 20th century, predating the digital era.

## Generative Approach

LeWitt's method involved creating rule-based systems and instructions that could generate multiple variations. Rather than directly executing every detail by hand, he established the generative framework and allowed the system (whether through assistants following instructions or through systematic principles) to produce the final work.

This approach makes LeWitt a precursor to digital generative art, though his systems operated through conceptual and manual processes rather than computation.
